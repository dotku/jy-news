She now joins the crowded field of celebrity-hosted podcasts, but podcast audiences have continued to expand in recent years, becoming increasingly diverse – especially as more podcasts adopt video formats and are distributed on platforms like Spotify and YouTube.

Podcast hosts have become opinion leaders in the political and cultural spheres, with a group of male hosts such as Joe Rogan, Theo Von, and Sean Ryan garnering large fan bases and frequently inviting prominent guests onto their shows. Former President Trump appeared on their programs during his 2024 presidential campaign, reaching young male audiences.

Regarding these male-leaning podcasts, Sharapova said, “I do listen to some.” She added that she listens to "All-In," a tech podcast hosted by four male tech investors, and the health podcast "Huberman Lab." But she believes there is still significant room for expansion in the market for female hosts.

“There are already some incredible women doing it, but there definitely aren’t enough,” Sharapova said.

A study by the Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism at the University of Southern California, analyzing 592 podcasts, shows that approximately two-thirds of podcast hosts are male, a proportion that is even higher in comedy, business, and technology podcasts.

Currently popular female-hosted podcasts include Amy Poehler’s “SmartLess” (ranked #4 on Spotify) and Alex Cooper’s “Call Her Daddy” (ranked #17).

However, the podcast audience is changing. A recent survey conducted jointly by Edison Research and SSRS shows that 62% of men and 54% of women listened to podcasts last month, up from 46% and 39% in 2023, respectively.

The first season of "Tough as Nails" will feature interviews with a number of guests, including comedian Chelsea Handler and NFL Chief Financial Officer Christina Doffey.

“I want to have deep conversations between women,” Sharapova said.

To prepare for her show, Sharapova said she studied the hosting styles of Oprah Winfrey and Amy Poehler, aiming to create a comfortable space where guests can speak openly.

Sharapova is an Olympic medalist and five-time Grand Slam champion who retired from professional tennis in 2020. She was known as the “Ice Queen” on the court for her aggressive playing style, and she says she often surprises people when she shows her sense of humor.

“I was almost limited to one persona in the past,” she said, adding that there are many other sides to her personality.

Over the years, Sharapova has also emerged as an investor, investing in skincare brand Supergoop!, wearable weight brand Bala, and serving as a director of high-end outdoor clothing brand Moncler.

Sharapova said she spoke with several potential partners before choosing Vox Media because they gave her creative autonomy over the content. Her initial deal includes a guaranteed income and a share of advertising revenue; other programs under Vox Media use different partnership models.

For example, "Pivot," hosted by Kara Swisher and Scott Galloway, has no upfront guarantee, but hosts receive a high percentage of the show's advertising revenue.

Vox Media also produces shows such as "Maintenance Phase," hosted by Aubrey Gordon and Michael Hobbes, and "More Than That," hosted by Sue Bird and Megan Rapinoe.

Vox Media CEO Jim Bankoff declined to comment on reports regarding the company’s consideration of selling its podcast business, but said he remains optimistic about the medium.

“Podcasting is deeply impacting almost every aspect of our lives,” he said of the podcast industry.

Of course, the most eye-catching aspect is its price: the 128GB entry-level version is priced at $249.99 (approximately RMB 1703.86), while the top-of-the-line 4TB version costs as high as $1799.99 (approximately RMB 12268.19), and all capacities are offered with a limited lifetime warranty.

With the top-end version priced at over 12,000 yuan, this storage card naturally has unique skills in video recording.

Models with 1TB and above capacity have obtained VPG-1600 certification, guaranteeing a sustained write speed of no less than 1600MB/s, which is sufficient to meet the demands of RAW video shooting at resolutions up to 12K.

Smaller capacity models have obtained VPG-400 and VPG-800 certifications respectively, ensuring that the storage card will not drop frames or interrupt recording due to write speed fluctuations when recording high-bitrate video.

This storage card is equipped with SmartIdle technology, which can intelligently switch to low-power mode when the device enters standby, effectively preventing overheating.

At the same time, its robust and durable design allows it to withstand drops of up to 5 meters and resist bending forces of up to 70 Newtons, ensuring data security even in harsh outdoor environments.

In terms of capacity selection, SanDisk offers six specifications ranging from 128GB, 256GB, 400GB, 1TB, 2TB to the top-of-the-line 4TB.

This new product is expected to be officially launched in the third quarter of 2026.

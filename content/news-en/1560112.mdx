Sharma explained in the interview how memory costs are affecting product pricing and supply capabilities. She stated: "All of this is an equation. Memory costs will affect pricing, and will also affect supply. When we think about the goal of 'a place for all players,' we have to consider these factors. Therefore, we are not yet able to announce a specific release schedule. The world is changing rapidly. However, my top priority is to focus on the areas we can control, and to build an excellent console that can run great games (including PC games)."

As the tech industry continues to invest heavily in artificial intelligence and the data centers it requires, demand for storage chips, RAM, and SSDs has surged, leading to a global memory shortage. Prices for various electronic products, from entry-level PCs to new TVs, have generally risen as a result. Interestingly, Microsoft itself is a core participant in the AI industry, meaning its AI business and Xbox business are inevitably competing for the same limited "memory cake."

Microsoft officially announced Project Helix at the Game Developers Conference in March of this year and confirmed that development kits will be distributed to game developers in early 2027. However, Microsoft has not given a clear commitment regarding the console's specific release date or release window. When asked about AMD's suggestion that Project Helix could launch in 2026, Sharma reiterated: "I can only say that our development kits will be released next year (2027). We are working very hard, and there is still a lot to do, and a lot to learn. But we are very excited about Project Helix and the initial feedback we have received."

As of now, the exact release date of Project Helix remains a mystery.

Netizen iruletheworldmo revealed information from the OpenAI team stating that GPT-6 will be released on April 14th. Pre-training was completed on March 17th, and post-training is also finished. Everything is ready for launch.

GPT-6 will also focus on significantly improving coding, reasoning, and agent task capabilities, with a performance increase of over 40% compared to GPT-5.4.

Native multimodality is also essential, with a single architecture for text, audio, images, and video.

Previously, OpenAI terminated the SORA video large model business and the $1 billion cooperation with Disney in order to fully invest in GPT-6. Even the product organization was changed to AGI Deployment, fully embracing the AGI era.

OpenAI executive Brockman stated that AGI has been achieved at 70-80%, and internally, it is believed that GPT-6 will fill the remaining gap, implying that GPT-6 will achieve AGI.

GPT-6's context window is 2M in size, twice that of GPT-5.4, and the price is $2.5/$12, which is the price per million input and output tokens, slightly more expensive than GPT-5.4 but still affordable.

In addition, with the launch of GPT-6, OpenAI's previously emphasized super application will also be realized, combining ChatGPT, Codex programming, and the Atlas browser into one powerful and comprehensive super intelligent agent.

In conclusion, although this leak comes from a netizen, the hype about AGI is consistent with OpenAI's usual style. However, the authenticity of this leak has been heavily criticized by other netizens, so let's take it with a grain of salt for now.

Regarding OpenAI's next product, previous leaks suggested GPT-5.5, which is considered more credible. There will definitely be improvements, but achieving AGI is likely still a long way off. The goal of AGI is now being overhyped by many AI companies.

However, OpenAI's claim of launching a super application seems more reliable, as it has been rumored before. ChatGPT, which only offers simple chat conversations, has limited significance. OpenAI needs to create an APP with greater user stickiness.

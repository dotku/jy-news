In terms of performance, the new car is equipped with a new dual-motor all-wheel drive system with a combined power of up to 850kW. Acceleration from 0 to 100km/h takes only 2.5 seconds, 0 to 200km/h takes 7.4 seconds, and the top speed can reach 260km/h.

The new car also adopts a direct oil-cooled motor derived from Formula E racing and a 600kW kinetic energy recovery system, with approximately 97% of daily braking completed by the electric braking system.

In terms of range and charging, the new car is equipped with a 114 kWh battery pack, supports 800V high-voltage fast charging, and takes only 16 minutes to charge from 10% to 80%. It is also equipped with an 11kW wireless charging function.

The exterior continues Porsche's all-electric family design language, with highly recognizable four-light strip daytime running lights, paired with an active air intake grille, 22-inch dense spoke wheels, and carbon ceramic brake system, maximizing the sporty atmosphere.

The rear adopts a new through-type taillight and diffuser shape, with tough lines, further enhancing the performance attributes.

The interior creates an immersive digital cockpit, equipped with a 12.25-inch curved OLED central control screen, a 14.6-inch full LCD instrument panel, and a 14.9-inch passenger entertainment screen.

Porsche retains a large number of physical buttons, balancing ease of operation, and also offers an optional 87-inch AR-HUD head-up display system.

Specifically for the Chinese market, the new car has been upgraded with multiple standard features, including the Porsche Active Ride chassis system, rear-axle steering, 22-inch high-performance wheels, AR-HUD, Burmester high-end audio, and front seat ventilation.

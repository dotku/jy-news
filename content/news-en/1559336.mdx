·Kiki Wolfkill joined Microsoft's gaming division in 1998 as an art director and joined 343 Industries (now Halo studio) in 2008. She served as executive producer for "Halo 4," "Halo: The Master Chief Collection," and related TV adaptations. In 2023, she became the head of Microsoft's Intellectual Property and Entertainment division.

·Kiki Wolfkill expressed great gratitude for the work opportunities provided by Microsoft, describing the 28 years as an amazing gift and experience, and offering sincere thanks.

·However, two weeks ago, Glenn Israel, art director for the "Halo" series, revealed internal bullying and illegal activities, sparking controversy. It is currently unknown whether Kiki Wolfkill's sudden departure is related to this matter.

A total of 17 games were tested, all at 1080p resolution with high or maximum settings, using an RTX 5090 graphics card to avoid any bottlenecks.

Average Performance: The 7800X3D led by over 10% in average frames and 6% in 1% low frames, a very significant advantage. Moreover, there's the 9800X3D above it, and the unreleased 9850X3D.

Average Frequency: The 270K Plus exceeded 5.2GHz, leading by a full 500MHz, but is comparable to the 9800X3D.

Average Power Consumption: The 7800X3D is a god-tier performer at just over 70W, while the 270K Plus approaches 110W.

Average Power Efficiency: The 7800X3D leads by a cliff-like margin, almost 70%!

Average Temperature: The 7800X3D runs slightly warmer at 64℃, but only by 5℃.

Looking at productivity performance, it's a completely different story.

In both the average single-threaded performance across 7 projects and the average multi-threaded performance across 10 projects, the 270K Plus took first place, while the 7800X3D unfortunately came in last in both.

Especially in multi-threaded performance, the 270K Plus leads by a large margin thanks to its more cores, exceeding by more than double.

In terms of productivity power consumption, randomly selected projects showed the same result: the 7800X3D consistently had the lowest, while the 270K Plus was about twice as high.

In terms of computing efficiency, the 7800X3D is significantly better.

The conclusion is simple:

If the prices are the same, which one to choose depends entirely on your personal use case.

If you play games a lot, choose the Ryzen 7 7800X3D without hesitation.

If your work is the primary focus, especially content creation, choose the Core Ultra 7 270K Plus without hesitation.

In addition, the Ryzen 7 7800X3D also has the advantages of low power consumption and high energy efficiency, and the AM5 platform has greater future upgrade potential.

After the race, founder Zhang Xue shared the winning moment on social media, overwhelmed with emotion: "Second championship, my heart can't take it!" This simple expression belies a 20-year journey from a motorcycle repair apprentice to the founder of a world champion racing team.

Breaking Barriers on the Track: A Crushing Victory for Chinese Manufacturing

WSBK vehicles must be modified from production models, serving as the ultimate test of a motorcycle’s true industrial capabilities. The middleweight class is particularly dominated by established powerhouses like the Ducati Panigale V2 and Yamaha YZF-R6, with Chinese brands previously unable to reach the podium. Zhang Xue Motorcycle’s 820RR-RS’s victory, leading the entire race and achieving a dominant win, not only broke the decades-long monopoly of European, American, and Japanese brands but also shocked the world with its overwhelming advantage of “nearly 4 seconds”—a significant margin in top-tier racing, where even 0.5 seconds makes a huge difference.

Remarkably, the speed of the Chinese motorcycle’s rise is noteworthy: Zhang Xue’s team finished 14th in the first race of the 2026 season in Australia. The team completed motorcycle tuning and technical optimization in just 30 days, achieving a rare and rapid comeback in the Portuguese race, a feat rarely seen in WSBK history.

A Legendary Grassroots Success Story

Behind the glory lies Zhang Xue’s almost “obsessive” passion. As the founder of Zhang Xue Motorcycle, he was born in 1987 in a rural area of Mayang County, Huaihua, Hunan Province, and dropped out of school at the age of 14 to become an apprentice at a motorcycle repair shop.

In 2006, at the age of 19, Zhang Xue rode an old motorcycle through muddy mountain roads for 3 hours and over 100 kilometers in the rain to chase after a reporter from Hunan Satellite TV’s “Evening News” program, hoping to secure an opportunity with a professional racing team. Soaked to the bone and freezing, with numb legs, he refused to give up.

The reporter was moved by his dedication and filmed his riding skills. After the program aired, he successfully joined a racing team—this “shameless” persistence became a turning point in his fate.

Over the next twenty years, he started as a stunt rider and mechanic, served as the product manager for Apollo Off-Road Factory, and became a core R&D member at Huanghe Motorcycle, leading the launch of the “Freedom 250X,” the first domestically produced motorcycle with electronic fuel injection priced under 10,000 yuan. In 2017, he co-founded Kaiyue Motorcycle, creating popular models like the 450RR, and led the team to compete in the 2023 Dakar Rally, achieving a historic breakthrough with “Chinese brand + Chinese rider + Chinese racing car” all on the same stage.

In 2024, due to disagreements with shareholders over his insistence on independently developing engines, Zhang Xue sold all his assets and founded a brand named after himself—Zhang Xue Motorcycle.

150 Horsepower Powered by Chinese Manufacturing

The 820RR-RS, the winning model in this WSBK race, embodies Zhang Xue Motorcycle’s technical strength: this model is equipped with a completely independently developed 819cc inline three-cylinder water-cooled engine, producing 150 horsepower in the racing version and a peak torque of 85N·m, with acceleration from 0 to 100 km/h in just 2.81 seconds. The vehicle’s dry weight is 168kg, more than 20kg lighter than its competitors, and features a twin-spar aluminum alloy frame and a forged magnesium alloy swingarm. It is also equipped with a six-axis IMU electronic control system, cornering ABS, 10-level TCS traction control, and bidirectional quick-shifter, along with top-tier components such as Brembo calipers, hlins suspension, and a Scorpion exhaust. Crucially, everything from the engine and frame to the electronic control system was independently developed by a Chinese team, completely shedding the label of imitation.

At the same time, the Zhang Xue Motorcycle team collected data on European tracks and repeatedly tuned the vehicle with international riders, using the stringent WSBK standards to improve civilian research and development. The standard 820RR is priced at 43,800 yuan, and the performance version at 61,980 yuan, creating a closed-loop system of track testing, technical iteration, and civilian application.

Behind the miracle lies the deep support of China’s motorcycle industry system and capital strength. In March 2026, Zhang Xue Motorcycle completed a Series A financing round, securing 90 million yuan in funding with a post-investment valuation of 1.09 billion yuan. Zhejiang Provincial Investment Group Fund led the investment, with Hangzhou Hongchang Technology indirectly participating through JinHua Zhechuang; intelligent networking company Hongquan Technology was granted strategic partner status, providing intelligent cockpit and electronic control system support. Tianyancha shows that the company’s registered capital has recently increased from 60 million to 65.4 million yuan, with Zhang Xue holding 73.39% of the shares.

In 2025, the company’s total output value was 750 million yuan, with R&D investment reaching 69.58 million yuan. Despite a full-year loss of 22.78 million yuan, it laid the foundation for technological breakthroughs. In 2026, seven new models will be launched intensively, with a target annual sales volume of 60,000 units and an output value of 1.8 billion yuan.

Initial Intentions and Controversy

Behind the championship glory, Zhang Xue Motorcycle also demonstrates a commendable sense of social responsibility. The 820RR model, due to its powerful performance (0-100 km/h in 2.81 seconds), the manufacturer proactively set a clause prohibiting purchases by drivers with less than one year of driving experience and established a reward mechanism for reporting violations: if a dealer sells the vehicle illegally, consumers who report it and are verified will receive a reward of 5,000 yuan. This measure was intended to ensure safety but was criticized for discriminating against beginners. After market regulators intervened, Zhang Xue responded frankly: “I’ll accept the fine, but my intentions were good.” Netizens rallied in support, saying, “It’s better to sell fewer cars and ensure safety. This is a company worth respecting.”

This unwavering commitment reflects Zhang Xue’s spirit—from the young man chasing reporters in the rain to the founder of a WSBK champion racing team, he has always prioritized passion over commerce. The company’s core values of “Extreme · Passion” on its official website are not just slogans but also actions: the entire engineering team are riders, and all supply chain partners are motorcycle enthusiasts.

In Conclusion

Zhang Xue Motorcycle’s victory is more than just a racing triumph. It is a microcosm of China’s manufacturing industry’s transformation from following to leading in originality, and a passionate chapter written by grassroots entrepreneurs rewriting industry rules. While Western media were still debating “whether China can build top-tier racing cars,” Zhang Xue Motorcycle has already answered with the roar of its engines.

When the world hears the roar of Chinese motorcycles, we see not only technological breakthroughs but also a nation’s pursuit of excellence and unwavering dedication to passion. The wheels of the future are rolling, and the track for Chinese manufacturing is becoming wider and wider.

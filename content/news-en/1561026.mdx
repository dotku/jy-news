Lawyers explain that these aftermarket additions, not originally designed for vehicles, are highly susceptible to strong winds and vibrations during high-speed driving. If they come loose and fall off, they can instantly become unexpected obstacles that following vehicles cannot avoid, easily causing chain collisions or rear-end accidents.

If such decorations fall off during driving and cause an accident, the vehicle owner who installed them is usually responsible for all damages and subsequent economic compensation. Furthermore, if the position of the added decorations obstructs the license plate, it will directly violate strict provisions of traffic regulations.

According to relevant laws, intentionally obstructing a vehicle's license plate is a serious violation. Offenders will not only face a fine of 200 yuan but will also have 9 points deducted from their driver's license. This practice of challenging the legal bottom line in pursuit of aesthetics often comes at a very heavy price.

Road traffic safety concerns everyone. While car owners pursue personalized expression, they must prioritize legality and safety. Privately adding any non-official configurations to the exterior of a vehicle constitutes illegal modification. Do not risk the safety of others and yourself for momentary attention.

Currently, traffic management departments across the country have begun to pay attention to this phenomenon. To ensure a clean and safe driving environment, it is recommended that vehicle owners who have already added such accessories remove them as soon as possible, return to normal driving order, and jointly maintain a civilized and safe road traffic environment.

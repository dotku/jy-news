Currently, the specific cause of the fire and the vehicle model are pending further investigation and verification by official authorities.

Professionals remind that if a new energy vehicle, hybrid, or range-extended vehicle emits smoke, smells burnt, or experiences abnormal heating, immediately park and turn off the engine, move away from the vehicle, and do not attempt to open the hood or extinguish the fire yourself to avoid secondary danger.

Call 119 to report the fire, clearly stating the vehicle type (pure electric/hybrid), the location of the fire, and the size of the fire, to facilitate accurate firefighting.

If there are other vehicles or people nearby, promptly remind them to evacuate to prevent the fire from spreading and causing greater losses.

Non-professionals should not use water to directly douse the battery compartment. Instead, use a dry powder fire extinguisher to spray the ignition point, prioritizing control of the fire's spread.

In this incident, bystanders promptly intervened to extinguish the fire, effectively curbing its expansion, but the driver's handling was clearly not calm and collected enough.

Last year, Saber Interactive's Chief Creative Officer Tim Willits believed that the studio's "World War Z" game (with 30 million players) may have helped convince Paramount executives to approve a sequel: "30 million people have played this game, that's insane. I guess Paramount talking about making a sequel is because of that."

The first "World War Z" was released in 2013, grossing over $540 million worldwide. The sequel has faced numerous obstacles: director David Fincher was once contracted to direct, but Paramount halted pre-production in 2019 due to budget concerns. It is currently unclear whether Brad Pitt will return for the sequel.

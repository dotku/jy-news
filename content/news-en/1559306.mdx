Half an hour later, with the help of her companions and staff, she struggled to stand up and put on the exoskeleton device. The woman, who had lost her normal walking ability, "stood" up again with the assistance of the device and slowly took a few steps.

Witnessing this scene, her companions were so moved that tears welled up in their eyes. According to the booth staff, this product is a waist-assisted exoskeleton, which mainly helps people with mobility impairments improve their walking ability. Its working principle is: by sensing subtle human movements to identify movement intentions, and then using motor force to assist the legs to complete walking movements.

In the service robot area, in addition to exoskeleton robots, there are also AI simultaneous translation glasses, patrol robots, automatic cleaning robots and other types of products.

They are no longer exhibits in the laboratory, but "best-selling" products that have been mass-produced, are perceptible and connectable, and have also become a new business card for China's intelligent manufacturing "going global."

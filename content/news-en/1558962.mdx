The four anodized aluminum lens protection plates exposed this time are labeled black, silver, wine red, and blue. However, it’s important to note that these are merely camera protection accessories made by third-party accessory manufacturers, not the body components of the entire device. Their design is intended to echo the rumored new color schemes. Industry consensus generally believes that the "black" label on the accessories is closer to the previously rumored dark gray tone, rather than a traditional pure black body. Another leaker, Shana Digital, who is also active on Weibo, previously claimed that Apple will not offer a black version for its new high-end models this year, meaning the iPhone 18 Pro series may miss out on the classic black option for the second consecutive year.

Reports indicate that these four color schemes are still in the development and evaluation stages, and there are months until the product launch. Apple still has room to adjust the final color scheme before mass production. As usual, the iPhone 18 Pro series is expected to be officially unveiled in September this year, when Apple will also simultaneously launch its first foldable iPhone, which will also have independent color planning. It is rumored that the body colors of the foldable iPhone will be more understated and restrained, with options including silver, white, and indigo already mentioned.

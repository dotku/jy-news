The iPhone 11 series, released in September 2019, and the iPhone SE 2, released in April 2020, have been discontinued for many years and are no longer sold by Apple, but Apple still provides major software and feature updates.

After iOS 27 is released, although these models will not be able to upgrade, Apple will continue to provide necessary security updates to fix vulnerabilities. Of course, continuing to update to the latest version may not be a good thing for these models, as performance issues may cause them to become increasingly laggy.

Some savvy users have even remained on the iOS 18 series and have not upgraded to iOS 26. For the iPhone 11 series and iPhone SE 2, staying on iOS 18 + replacing the battery should allow them to continue to be used for many years.

Of course, there will be some impact: some applications may have higher requirements for the iOS version. For example, X/Twitter's X Chat end-to-end encrypted chat application directly requires iOS 26 at a minimum, but mainstream applications usually offer higher version compatibility.

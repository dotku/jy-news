A major highlight of the screen is its support for physical 90-degree rotation, allowing users to freely switch between horizontal and vertical display modes according to their workflow.

The machine supports Split view split-screen display, which can automatically divide the screen into two independent workspaces, significantly improving multitasking efficiency.

The top of the display is equipped with a 16-megapixel AI camera that supports human detection, automatically sensing when the user leaves or returns to their seat.

The camera also features a deskview function, which can recognize paper documents within the field of view and convert them into digital data in real-time for subsequent AI processing.

The body complies with the MIL-STD-810H military standard and has passed 12 tests, and the panel section has IP55-level dust and water resistance.

In terms of core configuration, the ThinkCentre X AIO Aura Edition is equipped with an Intel Core Ultra X7 series processor, and can be optionally equipped with up to 32GB of LPDDR5X memory and a 4TB PCIe Gen 5.0 SSD.

The ThinkCentre X Tower adopts a 34-liter chassis design and is positioned for high-performance professional workloads. It supports up to an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5090 32GB discrete graphics card.

In terms of core configuration, this model is equipped with an Intel Core Ultra 200S Plus series processor and can be optionally equipped with up to 256GB of DDR5 memory.

Both products are designed for enterprise-level AI workloads, with the all-in-one focusing on flexible display modes and document digitization, and the tower workstation targeting professional scenarios with extreme computing power requirements such as AI development and content creation.

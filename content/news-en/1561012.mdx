In addition, according to official statements, the Lisuan Technology 7G100 GPU will be launched for sale on JD.com on May 20th, but the specific price is unknown.

Just recently, Lisuan Technology also obtained Microsoft WHQL certification, becoming the first company in China and the fourth in the world to obtain this top-level pass for GPU companies.

WHQL certification is strictly issued by the Microsoft Hardware Quality Lab. Passing the certification means that Lisuan's graphics cards and drivers have reached an official level of deep compatibility with the Windows system. This is the foundation for domestic graphics cards to truly enter the large-scale consumer market and achieve commercialization.

Lisuan Technology's previously launched 7G100 series GPUs are based on a 6nm process. Unlike the common licensing model on the market, this series of graphics cards are completely independently designed by Lisuan, from the computing core to the instruction set.

This series of GPUs is based on the self-developed TrueGPU Tiantu architecture, and is equipped with a self-developed instruction set and software stack. This approach of not relying on third-party IP licensing means that Lisuan Technology completely masters the underlying autonomy of the GPU architecture, avoiding potential technical sanctions risks.

In performance testing, the Lisuan eXtreme series graphics cards scored as high as 26800 in the FireStrike test, and also achieved a score of 2268 in the Steel Nomad test, which reflects overall strength.

By comparison, the mainstream NVIDIA model RTX 4060 scores approximately 2302 in the same mode.

In terms of actual gaming experience, this series of graphics cards running the domestic 3A masterpiece "Black Myth: Wukong" at 1080P resolution and high image quality settings can maintain an average frame rate of over 70 frames.

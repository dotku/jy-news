The specific new retail prices are as follows:

Disc version PS5: Increased from HK$3,780 to HK$4,730, an increase of HK$950

Digital edition PS5: Increased from HK$2,980 to HK$4,257, an increase of HK$1,277

PS5 Pro: Increased from HK$5,780 to HK$6,502, an increase of HK$722

PS Portal: Increased from HK$1,580 to HK$1,936, an increase of HK$356

Among these, the digital edition PS5 has the largest price increase, exceeding HK$1,200, while the disc version and PS5 Pro have price increases of nearly HK$1,000 and HK$700 respectively.

Sony explained the reason for the price increase in an official announcement: “Faced with continued pressure from the global economic situation, we have decided to raise the prices of PS5, PS5 Pro and PlayStation Portal remote play devices globally. We understand that price changes will affect the player base, but after careful evaluation, we believe this is a necessary step to ensure that we can continue to provide innovative, high-quality gaming experiences to players around the world.”

It is worth noting that Sony had already increased the prices of PS5 products in other regions on April 2nd of this year, and Hong Kong did not participate in the first round of price adjustments at that time. This price adjustment for the Hong Kong version can be seen as a completion and follow-up to the global price increase strategy.

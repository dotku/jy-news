  China Mobile Communications Group Shandong Co., Ltd. has issued a notice
  adjusting the fees for SIM card replacements and exchanges, clarifying that
  from May 7, 2026, the business of replacing and exchanging mobile SIM cards
  within the province will be fully charged, and free services will no longer be
  provided. The announcement shows that the fee standard will be differentiated
  according to card type, with a fee of 20 yuan per replacement/exchange for
  ordinary USIM cards and 25 yuan per replacement/exchange for Super SIM cards.

When handling replacement and exchange services through online channels, in addition to the corresponding card fee, postage fees will still be collected according to current standards, subject to the display on the processing page.

This policy is a regional adjustment for Shandong Mobile and is not a nationwide uniform policy for China Mobile.

Previously, China Mobile has implemented a fee for SIM card replacements and exchanges in provinces such as Shanxi and Henan, while regions such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Fujian have not yet released similar adjustment announcements.

Shandong Mobile users who need to know more details can call the customer service hotline 10086 for consultation or go to the local offline business hall to confirm specific processing rules and fee details.

Mobile users in other regions can log in to the China Mobile App and check the current fee standards and processing requirements on the replacement and exchange service page.

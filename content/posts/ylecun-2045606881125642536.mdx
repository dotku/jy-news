The tensor engine was first implemented inside SN3 (before it was called Lush) in 1992 at Bell Labs by Léon Bottom and me.

The naming convention has survived to this day in PyTorch and other libraries.



The naming of the tensor operations was reused in EBlearn (C++ deep learning library written by Pierre Sermanet and me, with some help from https://nitter.net/soumithchintala).

It was recycled in Torch5 and Torch7, which was written largely by Ronan Collobert, and my students  Clément Farabet, and https://nitter.net/koraykv ). Clément and Koray had been brought up on Lush (the open version of SN) and knew the nomenclature. 

Then, Soumith used the same conventions in PyTorch.
